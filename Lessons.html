INTRODUCTION

Nowadays, social media platforms are everywhere, linking us in many ways we never imagined. It enabled us to instantly and easily convey and exchange information with accounts of other individuals as well as get updates from what is happening in our country and of others. Although this instant sharing has numerous benefits, it also carries significant risks.

On social media, you can't always trust that what you see is true. Many people have been tricked by fake news. This fake news has made lots of people unsure about what to believe online. That's why it's really important to think carefully and check if something is true before you share and believe it.

This website is a helpful one for those who want to learn how to carefully analyze and evaluate the truthfulness of information seen online. It provides quick and easy recommendations on how to be cautious when browsing through the vast amount of content available online. This website teaches you critical abilities to distinguish between what is real and not, enabling you to share information to others without hesitation.


IMPORTANCE OF FACT CHECKING

What is Fact Checking?

Fact-checking is a process of investigating, evaluating, and researching the information to ensure that it is reliable and factual. These days, people are more likely to rely on information that is directly delivered to them without fully understanding whether it is accurate or not. This is because information may be easily manipulated. Verifying whether the content is reliable and to avoid spreading misinformation is the goal of fact-checking.

Why is Fact Checking Important?

The spread of information via social media, news, and educational purposes is particularly significant due to its easy transmission and ability to impact individuals' beliefs and behaviors. Since there could be consequences for publishing false substances, fact-checking is essential to ensure the content is accurate before sharing it with others.

  • To prevent the spread of false information; people can become confused by material lacking important context.
  • The ability to distinguish between false information, opinions, and facts.
  • Increase credibility.
  • Avoid potential ethical and legal issues.
  • To ensure the content is trustworthy and accurate.
  • Verifying the accuracy and validity of the information.
  • It can help in your understanding of ideas you are currently familiar with or would like to learn more about.

What is internet safety?

Internet safety refers to the practices and strategies used to protect people from online theft, fraud, and other digital threats. Achieving internet safety involves a combination of factors, including installing security software, being aware of online rules and threats, following safe browsing practices, and practicing good digital hygiene like creating strong and unique passwords.

As technology becomes an increasing part of our everyday lives — from how we communicate to how we use basic appliances (Internet of Things) — staying safe online is crucial to ensuring we navigate the world safely. Though new internet technologies bring great benefits, they also give rise to new cyber threats. That means that online safety awareness is crucial to defend against threats such as malware, like viruses.

Unfortunately, most people don’t prioritize online protection. Figures and facts about internet safety paint a worrying picture:

  • In 2022, there was a reported 61% increase in phishing attacks.
  • 50% of people reportedly use the same password for all of their accounts.
  • 81% of company data breaches are apparently caused by poor passwords.

Who we are

As members of the NSTP, we have the responsibility to spread awareness to people, especially to the youth who are frequent users of social media platforms. We recognize the potential dangers associated with the rise of fake content online, which often preys upon the vulnerability of young individuals. It is crucial for us to educate and empower them with necessary skills to distinguish between truth from fallacious information in the digital landscape. Moreover, we must cultivate an environment that promotes critical thinking and media literacy to mitigate the negative impacts of misinformation and ensure the responsible use of social media among the youth.

VISION

Our vision is to help young people navigate social media safely. We want to teach them how to spot fake news and think critically online. By doing this, we aim to create a community where everyone uses social media responsibly and can trust the information they see.
